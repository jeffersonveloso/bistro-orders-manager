# ADR-001: Area-Based Access Boundaries for Operational Hardening

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-13

## Context

Stage 6 of the Vó Ziluca roadmap focuses on operational hardening after the MVP and Phase 1 live integration groundwork. The current product already supports two kitchens, a salão summary, and live-sync exception handling, but it does not yet enforce internal user authentication or role boundaries.

The first operational risk to solve is straightforward: salão staff must not be able to change kitchen production by mistake. The user clarified that the first release of this phase should prioritize clear area-based boundaries over broader platform hardening. The chosen operating model is based on area access, not individual identity.

## Decision

Introduce area-based authenticated access as the first Operational Hardening release.

The system will provide separate authenticated entry points for `Kitchen 1`, `Kitchen 2`, and `Salão`, each protected by its own area PIN. Each area will land in its own operational surface. Kitchen areas will be able to act only on their own production flow. The salão area will be able to monitor order progress and acknowledge sync exceptions, but it will not be allowed to alter kitchen production states.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Boundary + Manager Override

- **Description**: Add the same area boundaries as the chosen approach, but include a manager role with full access across all areas and actions.
- **Pros**: Covers escalation scenarios and gives one role authority across the whole operation.
- **Cons**: Expands MVP scope, increases training needs, and adds more role behavior to define before the first hardening release.
- **Why rejected**: The immediate goal is to stop accidental cross-surface actions with the smallest possible product change.

### Alternative 2: Protected Salão Only

- **Description**: Restrict salão access and actions, while leaving kitchen-to-kitchen boundaries less strict.
- **Pros**: Fastest path to blocking the most visible error source.
- **Cons**: Leaves an incomplete boundary model because kitchens could still act outside their own scope.
- **Why rejected**: The user wants separate profiles for kitchen and salão and expects each kitchen to stay inside its own operational lane.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Reduces the most important day-one operational error: salão changing production by mistake.
- Gives each area a simpler, more trustworthy surface.
- Preserves the current kitchen-first workflow while adding a clear access model.

### Negative

- Does not provide individual operator traceability in the first release.
- Does not yet solve the broader Stage 6 items around backup guidance, observability, or real-time transport.

### Risks

- Area PIN sharing may reduce accountability.
- Operators may expect a manager override path that is not yet present.
- If surface boundaries are unclear, users may still be confused about where to perform an action.

## Implementation Notes

This ADR defines the product boundary for the first Operational Hardening slice only. Future ADRs may extend the model with individual identity, manager escalation, richer audit visibility, or additional operational safeguards if the pilot requires them.

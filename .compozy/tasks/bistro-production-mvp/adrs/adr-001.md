# ADR-001: Kitchen-First Synchronized Production Scope for V1

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-11

## Context

Vo Ziluca operates with two physical kitchens separated by a wall. Paper tickets create delays, misreads, and poor synchronization between both sides of the same customer order. The product idea is to replace that workflow with a digital production system that automatically splits order items by kitchen and keeps both sides aligned until the full order is ready.

The repository is currently greenfield apart from a Next.js 16 scaffold. The required implementation stack includes App Router, shadcn/ui, Tailwind CSS, Radix UI, TanStack Query, and SQLite. The future external provider is Anota AI, but the public docs available in this environment are partially JS-only, so provider details are not reliable enough to drive V1 scope.

Market and operating context support a focused kitchen operations MVP. National Restaurant Association research shows persistent labor pressure and continued restaurant investment in operational technology. Competitive KDS products consistently emphasize multi-station routing, expediter views, timers, and fast in-kitchen interaction. The main decision is how much scope and architecture to include in V1 without delaying validation of the real workflow.

## Decision

Build V1 as a kitchen-first synchronized production system, not as a general restaurant operations platform.

V1 will include:

- Automatic item split by kitchen based on explicit menu-to-kitchen mapping
- Kitchen-specific production tickets and status updates
- A shared order detail view that shows both kitchens' progress for the same order
- Fast touch-friendly status actions for kitchen staff
- A minimal read-only salão or expediter summary with only order identity and consolidated readiness
- An internal provider adapter boundary so seeded or mocked order ingestion can be replaced by Anota AI later

V1 will also include a small explicit domain and application core around the business rules that must survive future UI and provider changes:

- `Order`
- `OrderItem`
- `Kitchen`
- `ProductionTicket`
- `ProductionStatus`
- `MenuItemKitchenMapping`
- `SplitOrderService`
- `OrderSyncService`
- `OrderProviderPort`

This structure is the minimum correct architecture for the production split and synchronization rules. V1 will not include broader platform abstractions or speculative extension points outside those boundaries.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Strict kitchen-only MVP with no salão or expediter summary

- **Description**: Limit V1 to the two kitchen views and omit any consolidated handoff surface outside the kitchen.
- **Pros**: Smaller surface area, less UI work, lower risk of scope creep.
- **Cons**: Fails to validate the final handoff outcome where synchronized kitchen work becomes customer-facing value.
- **Why rejected**: A minimal read-only summary is small enough to include and directly tests whether the synchronization loop reduces handoff confusion.

### Alternative 2: Broader restaurant operations platform from day one

- **Description**: Include deeper provider integration, menu management, analytics, inventory, staff roles, and broader configurability in V1.
- **Pros**: More comprehensive product surface and a stronger long-term platform narrative.
- **Cons**: Delays validation of the core production workflow, increases failure modes, and risks coupling the model to the first provider or UI shape.
- **Why rejected**: The bistro's immediate pain is execution and synchronization between two kitchens, not platform breadth.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Focuses delivery on the highest-value operational loop
- Keeps the domain independent from Anota AI payload shapes
- Preserves enough structure to evolve safely after V1
- Allows rapid validation using seeded or mocked orders before production integration is complete
- Tests the kitchen-to-handoff flow end to end without expanding into a full atendimento module

### Negative

- V1 will not satisfy broader restaurant back-office expectations
- Some future integration details may require reshaping infrastructure once Anota AI specifics are fully known
- A read-only salão summary may still require careful wording to avoid status ambiguity

### Risks

- **Operational usability risk**: The UI may still be slower than paper under rush conditions.
  - Mitigation: optimize for large touch targets, minimal taps, legible status language, and distance readability.
- **State ambiguity risk**: Kitchen-ready and order-ready may be confused if statuses are poorly modeled.
  - Mitigation: separate kitchen ticket status from consolidated order status in the domain.
- **Coupling risk**: Provider payloads could leak into UI or domain code during implementation.
  - Mitigation: enforce a dedicated provider adapter and internal order model from the start.
- **Scope creep risk**: The minimal salão summary could expand into a second workflow surface.
  - Mitigation: keep it read-only in V1 and exclude editing, notifications, permissions, and kitchen internals.

## Implementation Notes

- Use seeded or mocked orders as the default ingestion path for MVP validation.
- Keep the production split and synchronization rules in domain or application services, not in UI components.
- Treat SQLite as the local source of truth for tickets, mappings, and synchronized state.
- Defer full provider sync semantics until the adapter contract is validated against the real integration path.

# ADR-003: Alert Only on Operationally Relevant External Changes and Route Resolution to Atendimento

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-05-11

## Context

The first live integration needs a clear exception-handling rule. After an order is imported, the provider may emit later changes. If the system alerts on every upstream delta, the kitchen board will become noisy and staff will start ignoring warnings. If ownership is unclear, alerts will be visible but not actionable.

The product already prioritizes Kitchen 1 and Kitchen 2 as the primary beneficiaries of automatic order intake. That means the board should stay production-focused while exception resolution is owned by the operational role closest to the customer and the external ordering source.

## Decision

In Phase 1, the system will alert only on operationally relevant external changes and will assign the primary resolution responsibility to atendimento or salão staff.

Operationally relevant external changes include:

- Provider-side cancellation of an imported order
- Item addition or item removal after import
- Quantity change after import
- Customer-facing notes or modifiers that change production requirements after import

The system will not alert on non-operational changes that do not affect kitchen production or customer handoff.

When such a change is detected:

- The order remains stable in the kitchen board
- The board shows a visible per-order exception marker
- Atendimento or salão becomes the primary operational owner of resolution
- The kitchen is informed through the board state, but does not own provider reconciliation

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Alert on every external change

- **Description**: Any upstream delta generates a visible warning.
- **Pros**: Maximum visibility and low risk of hiding provider behavior.
- **Cons**: High alert noise, lower trust, and reduced actionability during service.
- **Why rejected**: The first release needs focused exceptions, not exhaustive event exposure.

### Alternative 2: Make the kitchen own all divergence resolution

- **Description**: Kitchen staff receive and resolve provider-side changes directly.
- **Pros**: Keeps exception handling close to production.
- **Cons**: Pulls kitchen users into customer and provider reconciliation work, interrupting throughput.
- **Why rejected**: The kitchen should stay focused on execution, while atendimento handles external-order reconciliation.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Reduces alert fatigue during live service
- Makes exception handling operationally actionable
- Preserves kitchen focus while still surfacing relevant divergence

### Negative

- Atendimento must absorb a clearer operational responsibility
- Some provider-side low-impact changes will not be surfaced in Phase 1

### Risks

- **Risk**: The defined set of relevant changes may still miss a meaningful edge case.
  - **Mitigation**: Review exception logs during pilot and refine the alert set before broader rollout.
- **Risk**: Atendimento may not respond fast enough during rush periods.
  - **Mitigation**: Make order-level exceptions prominent in shared operational surfaces and training.

## Implementation Notes

- Treat exception handling as a defined workflow, not an informal warning.
- Keep the alert vocabulary understandable for kitchen and atendimento users in live service conditions.
